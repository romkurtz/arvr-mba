Regulation of Medical Training — USA

The regulatory landscape for medical professionals in the United States.

  • Regulation of medical training and licensure is managed at the state level
  • Each state sets the requirements for doctors, nurses, and EMT (emergency medical technicians such as paramedics)
  • Many states (but not all) require medical professional to undergo Continuing Medical Education (CME)
  • Each state has a regulatory body for each profession (e.g. the New York State Board of Nursing)
  • The state boards have national organizations promoting collaboration between them and their interest
Slide 3 / CME

CME in the USA

How Continuing Medical Education works across professions and states.

  • States require that CME credits be obtained from activities that have been accredited. The accreditation and standard setting is provided by associations, boards and other professional organizations (e.g. the American Medical Association - AMA)
  • Some of the associations have international collaborators, including in Israel.
  • CME can include many different programs, including courses and simulations
  • Medicine, nursing and EMS have different sets of state regulations, organizations and accreditations
  • Some hospitals provide CME for their staff, but additional offerings are available through various associations and through private providers
  • Some CME is available for free
